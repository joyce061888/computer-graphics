Project Overview


Creating an animated scene with Pokemon.

Project Statement

We are creating an animated scene with Jigglypuff, Snorlax, and Chinchou using core concepts of Computer Graphics such as hierarchical modeling, material and lighting, Bezier curves and surfaces, texture mapping, and animation.


Modular Design

In order to make our scene fault-tolerant, we decided to take a modular approach to our design. We created a js file for each of the three Pokemon frames we made and imported them into our pokemon scene html file. Similarly, we imported our heart js file and zzz js file (the 'zzz' symbol that indicated that Snorlax is sleeping) in our scene file.

  1. Jigglypuff

    2. We used hierarchical modeling to create each part of Jigglypuff in different frames. Then, we arranged all the individual frames relative to one another and put them into one frame. We used a Bezier surface for its hair, a sphere geometry for its body and limbs, circle geometries for its eyes and mouth, and cone geomtries for its ear.

  2. Snorlax

    3. We used sphere geometries for its head, body and limbs, each scaled in an axis to stretch the sphere, and used cone geometries for its ears. We used portions of the sphere geomtry using the phi and theta angle for the beige portion of the belly and face, and we used Bezier curves for its eyes and mouth.

  4. Heart

    5. We used Bezier surfaces to create the heart surface. We create the front half of the heart and clones the other halves and positioned/rotated them relative to each other.

  5. ZZZ

    6. We used Bezier lines to create 3 straight lines and positioned/rotated them using basic trigonometry to get the 'Z' shape.
